Ingredients of Guaranteed Great Game Day Chunky Crock Pot Chilli

  1. It's 1 1/2 lb of ground chuck or leaner ground meat.
  2. It's 1 of large green bell pepper, diced.
  3. Prepare 1 of large green zucchini, peeled or unpeeled & diced.
  4. Prepare 1 of large yellow, white or red onion, peeled & diced.
  5. It's 16-18 of baby carrots, cut in half lengthwise then diced or 1 large carrot, peeled & diced small.
  6. It's 2 of celery, cut in half lengthwise & diced.
  7. Prepare 1 can (46 oz) of can of tomato juice (you may not need all this).
  8. Prepare 4 of small cans of kidney beans, drained & rinsed (I use 2 dark red & 2 light red).
  9. You need 2 can (14 oz) of or so of diced tomatoes, undrained.
  10. You need 1 of small can of tomato paste, not puree (optional but adds alot of flavor).
  11. You need 2 tbsp of level tbs of baking cocoa powder, unsweetened.
  12. Prepare 1 tbsp of or 2 tbs of chilli powder, not chilli seasoning mix in an envelope.
  13. You need 3 tbsp of dried parsley flakes, u can use fresh also.
  14. Prepare 2 tbsp of or 3 tbs of dried oregano (I use 3).
  15. Prepare 1 tbsp of garlic powder, not garlic salt.
  16. Prepare 1 tbsp of course ground black pepper or regular ground black pepper if its all u have.
  17. You need 1 of salt, to taste but be careful not to use too much if using the bouillon cubes.
  18. It's 2 of each beef bouillon cubes, optional but it add alot of flavor.
  19. Prepare 1 tbsp of white sugar (optional).

Guaranteed Great Game Day Chunky Crock Pot Chilli step by step

  1. Brown ground beef in a skillet on stove, drain grease/fat, add to crock pot, add all herbs, spices, cocoa powder & bouillon cubes to crock pot also.
  2. Sauté carrots, onions, zucchini, celery, peppers on stove until half done or until semi-soft, drain any oil/grease & add to crock pot.
  3. Add tomato paste if u are using it, stir through with the the meat & veggie mixture in the crock pot, add undrained tomatoes, add beans & stir through again.
  4. Open can of tomato juice, add to crock pot making sure to cover all mixture in the crock pot but be sure not to over fill the crock pot, stir through to combine everything.
  5. Cook in crock pot on low for about 4-6 hours or less or maybe even longer until done... I think this all depends on the crock pot and how thick or thin u like your chilli... When done serve as in for the big game or any meal... If u like toppings I would recommend some, sour cream, shredded cheese, crackers, corn chips or even hot sauce....
