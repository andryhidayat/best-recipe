Ingredients of Split green pea soup

  1. It's 1 tablespoon of olive oil.
  2. You need 2 tablespoons of butter.
  3. Prepare 1 of Onion, diced.
  4. Prepare 3 stalks of celery, diced.
  5. It's 2 of bay leaves.
  6. Prepare 1/4 tsp of thyme leaves,dried.
  7. You need 3 of carrots, chopped.
  8. It's 4-6 cloves of Garlic, presses or finely minced.
  9. You need 1/4 tsp of Pepper.
  10. It's 6 cups of chicken or vegetable broth low sodium (add another cup if you want thinner broth).
  11. Prepare 1 lb of Green Split Peas (rinced).
  12. It's 1 tsp of liquid smoke optional for vegetarian version.
  13. You need 1/2 tsp of salt kosher or 1/4 tsp table salt (add after cooking if needed).
  14. You need of Optional meats you can use: (or combination of them).
  15. It's 6 slices of bacon.
  16. You need 2 of small ham hocks.
  17. You need 2-3 cups of diced leftover ham (which is all I use).

Split green pea soup instructions

  1. Turn the pressure cooker on to the SautÃ© function. Let it warm up and add the olive oil and butter..
  2. Add the onion, celery, bay leaves, and thyme. Cook, stirring occasionally, until onion starts to turn translucent.
  3. Add the carrots, garlic, (bacon or ham, if using), and pepper and cook for a minute.
  4. Add broth, hambone or ham hock, if using, (Add liquid smoke, if using, for vegetarian method). Let come to a simmer..
  5. Stir in split peas..
  6. Place the lid on the pot and lock it in place. Set the steam release knob to the Sealing position..
  7. Cancel the sautÃ© function and Choose Pressure Cook/Manual, and use the + or - or dial to choose 18 minutes. High pressure. It will take a few minutes for the pot to come to pressure..
  8. .
  9. When the cooking time has finished, let the pot sit undisturbed for 15 minutes to naturally release some of the pressure. Then turn the steam release knob to Venting to quick release the remaining pressure..
  10. When the pin in the lid drops down, carefully open the lid, facing away from you. Remove the bay leaves and the bone or ham hocks, if you used them..
  11. Taste and add the salt as desired. Serve hot. The soup will thicken quite a bit when it cools, that is normal..
